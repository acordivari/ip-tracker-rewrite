Your IP address can be used for…

This is information about your online identity. Luckily, personal info like your name and "exact" location cannot be determined through your IP alone. However, most websites log your IP when you visit them as a way to protect the online community from abusive users. If an individual commits a cybercrime, the authorities can subpoena that individual's internet service provider for specific info after using a resource like the ARIN database. In addition to your ISP, most companies like Google and Facebook also store your IP address. There are multiple methods that companies use to track your behavior, habits, and search terminology, including cookies (less popular now due to cookie-blocking), web beacons, embedded services like Google Analytics, and fingerprint scripts.

If tracking becomes frustrating or bothersome, there are some easily-accessible tools to skirt around the issue. A VPN lets you obfuscate your IP address by routing traffic through different servers. This is a common workaround for people who want to access familiar parts of the internet from different countries, like watching a streaming catalog that isn't available locally.
